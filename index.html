What Is the FICA Tip Credit?

The FICA Tip Credit is a federal tax credit under IRS Section 45B.

In plain English, it is tied to employer Social Security and Medicare taxes paid on certain employee tips. That matters because tips are paid to your employees, not kept by the restaurant, but the employer may still pay payroll taxes connected to those reported tips.

This is not a new gimmick, grant program, or special promotion. It is a real tax-credit area that has been around for years.

Whether it applies depends on your restaurant's actual situation. The point of this page is not to give tax advice or tell you that your restaurant qualifies. The point is simply this: if your restaurant has tipped employees, it may be worth a closer review.

Why It May Not Have Been Reviewed

This credit can fall between payroll and accounting.

Your payroll records, reported tips, tax returns, and accounting work may all exist, but that does not always mean the FICA Tip Credit has been reviewed separately.

That does not mean your accountant did anything wrong. Your accountant may be handling your normal tax work properly. This is just a more specialized area that may not be part of the usual annual tax process.

If your restaurant has tipped employees and those tips are reported through payroll, it may be worth asking whether this has been looked at specifically.
